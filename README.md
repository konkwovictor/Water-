# 💧 Water Supply & Sanitation Dashboard Analysis

## 🚀 Project Overview
This project presents a **data-driven analysis of water supply, sanitation infrastructure, and public health risk** across Sub-Saharan Africa using an interactive dashboard built in Excel.

The dashboard evaluates **water availability, disease incidence, sanitation coverage, and community satisfaction** across 10 countries, providing actionable insights for policymakers and development organizations.

---

## 🎯 Objective
The primary goal of this project is to:
- Assess **water accessibility and infrastructure distribution**
- Analyze **waterborne disease risk levels**
- Evaluate **sanitation facility coverage**
- Understand **community satisfaction vs actual health outcomes**
- Provide **data-backed recommendations for intervention and policy**

---

## 🌍 Geographic Scope
The analysis covers **10 Sub-Saharan African countries**:
- Kenya
- Rwanda
- Nigeria
- Malawi
- Zambia
- Ethiopia
- Uganda
- Senegal
- Ghana
- Tanzania

## 🧠 Methodology

A structured **5-step analytical framework** was applied:

### 1. Data Identification
- Extracted all KPIs and visuals from the dashboard
- Catalogued metrics across countries and regions

### 2. KPI Extraction
- Recorded key indicators:
  - Population
  - Maintenance cost
  - Disease rate
  - Water availability
  - Satisfaction index

### 3. Comparative Analysis
- Ranked countries by:
  - Disease incidence
  - Water availability
  - Sanitation facilities
- Identified top and bottom performers

### 4. Risk Classification
- Analyzed disease risk levels:
  - LOW risk
  - MEDIUM risk
- Mapped countries into risk categories

### 5. Insight Generation
- Synthesized findings into actionable insights
- Developed prioritized recommendations

## 📊 Dashboard Walkthrough

### 💧 Water Supply Insights
- Regional water availability varies significantly
- Western Kenya leads with the highest availability
- Some regions lag behind, indicating infrastructure gaps

---

### 🦠 Disease Risk Analysis
- Malawi records the highest disease incidence
- Several countries fall within **medium risk levels**
- Indicates need for targeted health interventions

---

### 🚽 Sanitation Infrastructure
- Kenya leads with the highest number of facilities
- Ghana and Tanzania have the lowest coverage
- Highlights infrastructure inequality

---

### 😊 Community Satisfaction
- Average satisfaction: **72.8%**
- Highest in Kenya despite higher disease rates
- Suggests **perception vs reality gap**

---

## 🔍 Key Insights

### 🦠 1. Malawi Has Highest Disease Risk
- Incidence rate: **3,363.2**
- Requires urgent public health intervention

---

### 💧 2. Western Kenya Leads in Water Availability
- ~35% above regional average
- Strong model for replication

---

### 🚽 3. Infrastructure Inequality Exists
- Kenya: 230 facilities
- Tanzania: 168 facilities
- Significant disparity across countries

---

### 😊 4. Satisfaction ≠ Health Outcome
- High satisfaction does not correlate with low disease rates
- Indicates flawed perception metrics

---

### 💰 5. High Maintenance Costs
- $25K+ per facility annually
- Potential inefficiencies in resource allocation

---

### ⚠️ 6. Limited Risk Classification
- Only LOW and MEDIUM risk levels exist
- No HIGH risk category → limits monitoring capability

---

## 💡 Strategic Recommendations

### 🔴 1. Emergency Response (Malawi)

### 🔵 2. Replicate High-Performing Regions

### 🔵 3. Expand Sanitation Infrastructure

- Reduce infrastructure gaps

---

### 🟡 4. Optimize Maintenance Costs
- Conduct cost-efficiency audits

---
